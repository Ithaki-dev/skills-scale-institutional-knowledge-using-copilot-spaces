# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## QA/Test Engineer

### Role Summary
QA/Test Engineers ensure software quality through systematic testing and validation. They design test strategies, automate test execution, and provide quality feedback throughout the development lifecycle.

### Responsibilities
- Design and implement test plans; automate and execute test cases
- Validate features against acceptance criteria; coordinate regression and exploratory testing
- Provide test results and feedback to developers and PMs
- Identify quality risks and advocate for fixes before release

### Goals
- Catch defects early in the development cycle
- Maintain high test coverage and quality standards
- Reduce production incidents through thorough pre-release validation

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- Test result reports and bug triage meetings
- Quality status updates in sprint reviews

### Interacts with
- Developers (collaborate on test coverage), Project Manager (report quality status), Product Manager (clarify acceptance criteria), UX/UI Designer (validate usability)

---

## UX/UI Designer

### Role Summary
UX/UI Designers create user-centered designs that balance user needs, business goals, and technical constraints. They produce wireframes, prototypes, and design systems that guide feature implementation.

### Responsibilities
- Design wireframes, mockups, and user journeys
- Maintain UX and accessibility standards; participate in design reviews
- Validate feature usability pre-release; provide design assets to developers
- Conduct user research and usability testing

### Goals
- Maximize user satisfaction and product usability
- Ensure consistent, accessible user experiences
- Balance user needs with business and technical feasibility

### Typical Communication
- Design reviews and critique sessions
- User research findings and persona updates
- Design handoff meetings with developers

### Interacts with
- Product Manager (requirements & user needs), Developers (handoff assets, clarify implementation), QA (validate usability), Business Analyst (understand business requirements)

---

## Release Manager

### Role Summary
Release Managers coordinate and oversee the deployment of software to production environments. They manage release timelines, orchestrate cross-team activities, and ensure smooth rollouts with minimal risk.

### Responsibilities
- Coordinate release preparation and deployment; manage release calendar
- Communicate release status to stakeholders; ensure artifacts and documentation are ready
- Supervise rollout and post-release validation
- Coordinate rollback procedures if critical issues arise

### Goals
- Deliver releases on schedule with minimal disruption
- Minimize release-related incidents and rollbacks
- Maintain transparency and stakeholder confidence

### Typical Communication
- Release planning meetings and go/no-go reviews
- Deployment status updates and stakeholder notifications
- Post-release retrospectives

### Interacts with
- Developers (deployment readiness), Project Manager (release planning), Operations (support rollout), QA/Test Engineer (confirm quality gates), Stakeholders (update on release status)

---

## Operations/Support

### Role Summary
Operations and Support teams monitor production systems, respond to incidents, and provide technical support to users. They bridge the gap between development and end-users, ensuring system reliability and capturing production feedback.

### Responsibilities
- Monitor deployed systems; handle incidents, outages, and support requests
- Capture production issues and relay feedback to teams
- Update knowledgebase with support runbooks
- Participate in on-call rotations and incident response

### Goals
- Maintain high system availability and reliability
- Minimize mean time to resolution (MTTR) for incidents
- Provide excellent user support and escalation

### Typical Communication
- Incident reports and post-mortems
- On-call handoffs and escalation coordination
- Weekly operations reviews and metrics sharing

### Interacts with
- Developers (escalate production bugs), Release Manager (coordinate incident response), Project Manager (report operational risks), Stakeholders (communicate status and recovery actions)

---

## Business Analyst

### Role Summary
Business Analysts bridge business stakeholders and technical teams by gathering requirements, analyzing business needs, and ensuring solutions deliver measurable business value. They translate strategic goals into actionable features.

### Responsibilities
- Gather and document business requirements; map business goals to technical solutions
- Validate feature alignment with business needs
- Support backlog grooming and prioritization
- Create business cases and ROI analyses for proposed features

### Goals
- Ensure solutions deliver measurable business value
- Reduce rework through clear, validated requirements
- Bridge communication gaps between business and technical teams

### Typical Communication
- Requirements gathering sessions with stakeholders
- Backlog refinement and sprint planning meetings
- Business impact analysis presentations

### Interacts with
- Product Manager (requirements); Project Manager (scheduling, dependencies); Developers (provide detailed requirements); Stakeholders (clarify business value)
